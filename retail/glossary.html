E-commerce Glossary

Catalog

A comprehensive list of all the items that a store carries. Highest level of abstraction in Catalog Management system and consists of categories which in turn include Products.

Category

A category represents a set of Products. Example: Pets, Beauty, etc. Can have subcategories as well.

Product

A Product represents the general description of an item that can be sold and is made up of SKUs. For example, a Shirt is a Product and different colors will represent different SKUs.

SKU (Stock Keeping Unit)

A unique variation of the Product, for example, a white shirt.

SkuBundle

A collection of SKUs. Example: table and chairs.

Product Review

Reviews provided by customers of the Product. Products with higher ratings will have better chances of being purchased by the customers.

Pricing

Products and SKUs will have a price associated with them.

Promotion

Discounts associated with purchasing an item.

  • Item Level
  • Order Level
  • Shipping Level
  • Tax Level

Shipping

The process of delivering the item to the end users. Shipping Costs will vary depending on the type of Product being purchased.

Taxation

Each state might have different regulations on Taxation and goods sold will be taxed according to the jurisdiction.

Profile

In terms of an Ecommerce Store, refers to Customers who are the buyers of the item.

  • Guest - A person who purchases the item without creating an account on the Ecommerce site.
  • Registered User - A person with an account on the Ecommerce site and whose data will be stored on the Store's System.

Rewards

Businesses will have loyalty programs to attract customers.

Store

A physical location where the items can be purchased in person.

Cart

A Cart refers to an Object that will hold the items the user intends to purchase.

Order

Once the customer gives the payment information and completes the purchase, the Cart will be treated as an Order.

Abandoned Cart

A Cart which has items added but the user didn't proceed with placing the Order and leaves the website.

Payment

Different types of payment options which can be used by the Customer to purchase the item such as credit card, Paypal, Giftcard or Buy Now and Bill Me later or Leasing.

Wishlists

A collection of items that a customer is wanting to purchase or intends to purchase.

BOPIS

Buy Online and Pickup in Store. Retailers that have a physical location as well as an Online presence will provide this option so that users can save on Shipping Costs.

SDD

Same Day Delivery. Another way of attracting customers to purchase items.

Personalization

A way of attracting customers based on their purchase history or their interests.

CMS

Content Management Systems. These help in managing the catalogs of the Ecommerce site such as creating the Catalogs, Categories, Products, adding images for the Products and SKUs to be shown on the website.

OMS

Order Management Systems. These systems receive the Orders, help in fulfilling the Order, Tracking the Orders and inventory management and returns.

Brick and Mortar Retailers

Retailers that have physical store locations.

Online Only Retailers

Pure play Online retailers without any physical store presence.

POS

Point of Sale.

SEO

Search Engine Optimization. It is the process of optimizing the content of webpages of an ecommerce site so that they rank higher in search engines like Google.
