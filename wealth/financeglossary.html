Credit

An arragement to make payment in future for goods bought or loan taken.Maintain good credit by spending responsibly and not taking unnecessary debt.

Debt

Money borrowed from credit agencies such as for credit card, education loan, mortgage, etc.

Savings

Keeping aside the money that might be required for rainy days or retirement. The more you save during early years of work, the more you can have during retirement.

Budget

Keep a tab on your finances by analyzing the spending on credit cards, bank statements.

Invest

Invest part of the money that you save. Money can be invested in Real Estate (Home, land, etc.), precious metals like Gold, Silver, and in stock markets (Invest responsibly).

Nominate

Always nominate your beneficiary in investment accounts.

Rule of 72

A method used to calculate the time needed to double your investments. More details.

FDIC

Federal Deposit Insurance -> More details. Always check if the bank you are dealing with is FDIC insured or not. Investing in stocks and mutual funds is not FDIC insured.

IRA

Individual Retirement Account. Money invested in traditional IRAs is tax deductible. When money is withdrawn, tax needs to be paid.

Roth IRA

Money contributed to Roth IRA is not tax deductible. But money grows tax-free, and withdrawals are not taxed. Dividend-paying stocks are ideal for Roth IRAs.

BDC

Business Development Company. These companies provide loans to small- to mid-sized companies. BDCs are expected to distribute the majority of their earnings to shareholders and generally pay good dividends. Some good BDCs are MAIN and ARCC.

REIT

Real Estate Investment Trusts. They own, operate, or finance income-producing real estate. They are supposed to distribute 90% of their taxable income to shareholders. More details.

Float

Float refers to the number of shares that are available to be publicly traded. A low float stock can potentially have wide fluctuations in price.

EPS

Earnings per share. Calculated by dividing the net income by the number of shares. More details.

PE Ratio

Price to Earnings Ratio, calculated by dividing the current share price by earnings per share. Helps in measuring the relative value of a company against its peers. More details.

Debt to Equity Ratio

Value calculated by total liabilities divided by shareholder equity. More details.

Sector

Sector in terms of investing refers to the industry in which the company operates, for example, biotechs, utilities, consumer staples, technology, etc.

Diversification

Refers to investing across sectors so that risks associated with investing can be minimized.
